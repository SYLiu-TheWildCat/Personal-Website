Reading Seminar Fall 2022:

Symplectic Geometry of Algebraic Varieties

Introduction.  This seminar aims to understand recent progresses on symplectic geometry of algebraic varieties. The relation between symplectic and singularities was originally figured out by Varchenko, who showed that given a polynomial $f\colon\mathbb{C}^{n+1}\to\mathbb{C}$ with isolated singularities, the link $\mathbb{S}_{\varepsilon}\cap V(f)\subseteq\mathbb{S}^{2n+1}$ admits a natural contact structure, and this contact structure is in fact an algebraic invariant: if we have a holomorphic change-of-coordinates $F\colon\mathbb{C}^{n+1}\to\mathbb{C}^{n+1}$, then the links of $f$ and $f\circ F$ are contactomorphic. This leads us to a question: can we figure out more connections between the algebraic invariant of the singularity of $f$ and the symplectic invariant of the link $K(f)$, or alternatively, the symplectic invariant of the complement $\mathbb{C}^{n+1}\setminus V(f)$? There're many works computing various kinds of Floer-type homologies in this setting. On the other hand, we can regard the singular part as a divisor "at infinity", i.e. we consider a pair $(M,D)$ where $M$ is compact symplectic and $D$ is some normal crossings divisor(this is always the case by Hironaka's resolution of singularities), and consider the complement $M\setminus D$. This is a non-compact symplectic manifold, and if we pick $D$ to be ample, then $X=M\setminus D$ is exact. In this case we can consider the symplectic cohomology and (partially) wrapped Fukaya category associated to $X$, as well as "relative symplectic invariants" associated to the pair $(M,D)$, e.g. the relative Fukaya category [20], or log Gromov-Witten invariants [11].

Several papers are collected in the references list. Other suggestions are also welcome.
