Research

My research interest is symplectic geometry, in particular computations of symplectic invariants, e.g. symplectic cohomology, (partially) wrapped Fukaya category, in good cases, with the hope of getting some new symplectic topological results. Here're descriptions of my research projects.

  1. For a polarized (real) hyperplane arrangement $\mathcal{V} =(V\subseteq\mathbb{R}^n,\eta ,\xi)$, [BLPW] constructed $A$- and $B$-algebras associated to this data. A conjecture by [LLM] said that the $B$-algebra associated to $\mathcal{V}$ is isomorphic to the partially wrapped Floer homology of the bounded above regions in the complement $V\setminus\{x_1x_2\dotsb x_n=0\}$, and this algebra generates the partially wrapped Fukaya category of the complexfied complement $M(V)=V\otimes\mathbb{C}\setminus\{x_1\dotsb x_n=0\}\subseteq V\otimes\mathbb{C}$ with some suitably chosen stop at infinity. This conjecture was verified by them using a previous generation result by [A], and some combinatorial computations, in the case when $\mathcal{V}$ is cyclic. We are now working on generalizing this to all hyperplane arrangments in general position. This is a joint work in progress with Sukjoo Lee, Yin Li and Cheuk-Yu Mak.
  2. This is a side project to the above. When we want to apply sectorial descent in [GPS2] to glue sectors together and prove generation, we need to verify that the cover $X=X_1\cup\dotsb\cup X_n$ is sectorial, which means that the sectorial boundaries $\partial X_i$ should be mutually symplectically orthogonal and the corresponding $I$-functions commute. This turns out to be a very frustrated condition to verify, and breaks in many cases. In this work, we modify the argument from [McL] to show that, after possibly moving these sectorial boundaries, we can make them symplectically orthogonal. This is a joint work in progress with Sheel Ganatra.
